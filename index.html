Sliding Panel Project

Bathroom Washer/Dryer — 4 Panel System

Rail extends left into alcove stack zone. Panels open across full doorway width (~72"). Stack to left (washer side) away from dryer door swing.

Rail total length ≈ 90". Panels ≈ 15–18" each depending on measured opening.

Single piece of linen folded at top. Uniform topstitch on all 4 sides. Two aluminum+steel bar assemblies inside top and bottom channels.

Sew vertical sides first, then top and bottom channels so corners lock cleanly.

Half-inch overlap between adjacent panels. Neodymium magnet on trailing panel's steel bar attracts to steel bar on leading panel. Top and bottom at each joint.

All bars same profile: ¾" × ⅛". Steel and aluminum butted end-to-end inside channel — no epoxy needed between them.

Carrier pin drops through drilled hole in aluminum top bar. Washer-back eyelet set through both fabric layers and bar hole simultaneously — structural and decorative.

Washer-back eyelet locks through fabric + bar simultaneously — structural and a considered design detail.

⚠ Measure opening and confirm carrier pin diameter before purchasing rail, eyelets, or cutting any fabric.
